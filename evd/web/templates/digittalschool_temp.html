Bringing a school to every child's home.

Run an eVidyaloka Digital School that benefits the children of rural India and has everything a child looks for in a school experience. Whether you are an enterprising individual wanting to make a difference in the lives of young children of rural India or an NGO that works at the grassroots, eVidyaloka invites you to this mission of every child having a school in his/her pocket.

What is an eVidyaloka Digital School ?

An eVidyaloka Digital School is an outside-school learning opportunity and intervention. The highlight is the multi-mode approach: children learn from TV lesson broadcasts, interactive printed workbooks and worksheets, and an online app.

Enrollment to the program is absolutely free.

BE A RURAL EDUCATION CHAMPION.

Become an eVidyaloka Digital School Partner (DSP) and run a Digital School for Grades 5-10 in your Block, Taluka or Mandal. Be a Rural Education Champion.

What can students do in a eVidyaloka Digital School ?

Work on workbooks developed by experts
Access rich content (including video lessons) in their regional language as a TV broadcast and on the App
Interact with classmates and teachers
Have a friend circle
Attend a wide range of co-curricular courses
Submit assignments

Who can enrol in the school ?

Any student from Grade 5 to 8 belonging to the Karnataka State Board, Tamil Nadu State Board, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana State Boards, Maharashtra State Board and Hindi language based State Boards can enroll. Lessons based on respective state board syllabus will be available.

The students need not belong to any one physical/regular school.

Children who are not registered with any school can also enrol for the eVidyaloka Digital School. In fact, we encourage DSPs to get more such children into the eVidyaloka Digital School.

What is the role of a Digital School Partner (DSP) ?

Promote the program broadcast on TV and increase viewership and uptake.

Track and monitor student enrollment and related impact.

Facilitate the uptake of workbooks and worksheets.

Engage on the adoption of the app.

Facilitate the add-on services.

* The DSP will receive a kit from eVidyaloka to help him/her reach more students.

Who can be a Digital School Partner ?

The eVidyaloka Digital School can also serve as a livelihood opportunity. A DSP can be an organisation or individual. The eligibility criteria is as follows:
  • Local resident.
  • At least 18 years of age.
  • Graduate.
  • Have a reach of at least two villages.
  • Women preferred.
  • NGO with a reach of one block, taluka or mandal, experience of community activity, and focus on education and related programs.
  • Any organizations engaged in community support and service like cable TV operators, etc.
